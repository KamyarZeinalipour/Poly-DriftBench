📖 Annotation Guidelines

Poly-DriftBench — Human Quality Evaluation

1. Overview

You will evaluate 30 AI-generated conversations between a simulated user and an AI assistant. These conversations span different domains (IT support, legal advice, travel planning, etc.) and lengths (short, medium, long).

Your goal is to assess how realistic and high-quality each conversation is, as if it were a real interaction between a human user and an AI chatbot.

2. What to Evaluate

For each conversation, you will rate 5 dimensions on a 1-5 scale:

Dimension 1: Naturalness

Does this conversation sound like a real user-assistant interaction?

ScoreMeaning
1Completely unnatural — robotic, formulaic, obviously generated
2Mostly unnatural — some realistic moments but overall stilted
3Mixed — parts feel real, parts feel artificial
4Mostly natural — minor issues but generally believable
5Fully natural — indistinguishable from a real conversation

Dimension 2: User Realism

Does the user behave like a real person? Look for frustration, confusion, pushback, tangents, and personality.

ScoreMeaning
1User is robotic — always compliant, no personality
2User shows minimal personality — overly cooperative
3User has some personality but still feels scripted
4User feels realistic — shows frustration, asks follow-ups
5User is highly realistic — has genuine personality, pushes back

Dimension 3: Coherence

Does the conversation flow logically? Do topics connect? Does the assistant remember context?

ScoreMeaning
1Incoherent — topics jump randomly, no logical flow
2Mostly incoherent — frequent topic breaks or contradictions
3Somewhat coherent — general flow with some inconsistencies
4Mostly coherent — good flow with minor issues
5Fully coherent — smooth, logical progression throughout

Dimension 4: DDM Compliance

Check if the assistant's responses follow these format rules:

  • L1 — Acknowledgment: Each assistant response should contain [SYS_ACK: ACTIVE]
  • L2 — Structure: Responses should use numbered bullet points (1. 2. 3.)
  • L3 — Actionable: Advice should be specific and actionable, not vague
  • L4 — Citations: Claims should reference sources with [Source: ...]
ScoreMeaning
1No compliance — most rules violated
2Low compliance — some rules followed inconsistently
3Moderate — most rules followed with notable gaps
4High — nearly all rules followed, minor misses
5Full compliance — all 4 rules consistently followed

Dimension 5: Overall Quality

Your holistic impression — would this conversation be useful for evaluating AI models?

ScoreMeaning
1Not usable — too many problems
2Poor quality — significant issues
3Acceptable — usable but with clear weaknesses
4Good quality — minor issues only
5Excellent — high quality, suitable for research

3. Tips

  • Read the full conversation before rating — scroll through all messages
  • Pay attention to long conversations — quality may degrade toward the end
  • Use the Notes field for any specific issues you notice
  • You can go back and change previous annotations at any time
  • Your progress is auto-saved — you can close the browser and resume later

4. Estimated Time

The full annotation should take approximately 1.5–2 hours. Short conversations take ~2 minutes, medium ~4 minutes, and long ~6 minutes each.
